## Grammar Points

In order to keep the size of this page reasonable, I have moved most of the information on declensions to [another work-in-progress page](/r/learns/kazakh). Commonly seen declensions and conjugations are listed below, singular usage will be inline under the appropriate gloss. Within the notes for each gloss, some of the Kazakh may be written variously in capitals, like `ГАн`. This indicates that `Г` and `А` will change based on the consonants and vowels the word they are attached to (this is called consonant assimilation and vowel harmony, respectively). 

[accusative](/r/learns/kazakh#accusative) (<abbr>ACC</abbr>)
: object of a verb

[converb](/r/learns/kazakh#converb) (<abbr>CONV</abbr>)
: the converb is a marker to indicate that the verb in question is modified by an auxiliary to the main verb which supplies information like aspect (how a verb functions relative to time)

[dative](/r/learns/kazakh#dative) (<abbr>DAT</abbr>)
: the indirect object of the verb, e.g. to whom/what

[genitive](/r/learns/kazakh#genitive) (<abbr>GEN</abbr>)
: possessor of something marked with the posessive

[imperative](/r/learns/kazakh#imperative)<cite>RM §1.3.5, 26</cite> (<abbr>IMP</abbr>)
: verbal mood that indicates commands ('Clean your room!')

[instrumental](/r/learns/kazakh#instrumental) (<abbr>INST</abbr>)
: the noun with which the verb is performed

[locative](/r/learns/kazakh#locative) (<abbr>LOC</abbr>)
: the location of a clause (e.g in my mind)

[possessive](/r/learns/kazakh#possessive) (<abbr>POSS</abbr>)
: a noun possessed by another noun, commonly used in other grammatical functions

[present tense](/r/learns/kazakh#present-tense) (<abbr>PRS</abbr>)
: the verb is either currently going on or will happen in the future

## Glosses

--GLOSS--
Сонымен, білемін əркімнің таңдауы бар,
thus, know-{PST}-{1SG} everyone-{GEN} choice-{VN}-{POSS-3} exist
{!} So, I know that everyone has a choice,
{!} So, I know that everyone has a choice,
--ENDGLOSS--

* This sentence uses a nonfinite noun clause, i.e the "that" in the phrase "know that". These types of clauses are marked with either `+ГАн`, `+АтІн`, or `+у`.<cite>RW §1.5.1</cite> In this case, it's marked with `+у`, represented by <abbr>VN</abbr>, as it is the verbal noun marker.  
* Within the clause, there is an existential sentence, using the <abbr>GEN</abbr> + <abbr>POSS</abbr> form to mark the subject and object, respectively.<cite>RW 4</cite>


--GLOSS--
Ал менде арман бар,
but {1SG.LOC} dream existant
{!} But, I have a dream
{!} But, I have a dream
--ENDGLOSS--

* Similar to [above](#leipzig-line-1), this is an existential sentence, but instead it uses the [locative](/r/learns/kazakh#locative) to indicate the dream's existence.<cite>RW 4</cite>

--GLOSS--
Солай де, білемін сенің де таңдауың бар,
so {EMPH}, know-{PRS}-{1SG} {GEN.2SG} also choice-{VN}-{POSS-3} exist
{!} I know that you have a choice
{!} I know that you also have a choice
--ENDGLOSS--

* See [line 1](#leipzig-line-1).

--GLOSS--
сенде арман бар
{LOC.2SG} dream existent.
{!} And you have a dream
{!} You have a dream
--ENDGLOSS--

* See [line 2](#leipzig-line-2). There is no explicit "and" in the Kazakh.

--GLOSS--
Сенбеген таптайды, сен құлағанда тайып,
believe-{NEG}-{PTCP} trample-{PRS}-{3}, {NOM.2SG} fall-{ГАндА} slip-{CONV},
{!} The one who does not believe in you, will trample you, as soon as you strumble
{!} The one who does not believe will trample you, after you slip and fall
--ENDGLOSS--

* `сенбеген` is marked as a headless relative clause, using `+ГАн`, thus it has no explicit subject.<cite>RW §1.6.4</cite>
* `+ГАндА` is a method of expressing a temporal relationship between clauses, in this case, between the trampling and the falling clause.<cite>RW 48</cite> Because the subordinate clause (falling) is telic (completed) and punctual, the main event (trampling) occurs after the subordinate clause is complete.<cite>RW 50</cite>
* `таю:to slip`, verbs whose stems end in `й` are spelled with `+ю` rather than `+у` in the dictionary form.<cite>MEREY</cite>
* `+Іп` adverbializes the verb, thus `тайып:slippingly`.<cite>RW 256</cite>

Бірақ сенуге əрдайым мендік жүрек дайын!
but believe-{VN}-{DAT} always {1SG.NOM}-{ЛІк} heart ready
{!} But, my heart continues to believe
{!} But, for believing, my heart is always ready
--ENDGLOSS--

* A verb noun plus the [dative](/r/learns/kazakh#locative) indicates a nonfinite purposive clause, i.e. "for the purpose of verbing".<cite>RW §1.11</cite>
* `əрдайым:ready` is also given as "pretty often" or "more often than note", but neither of those are as inspiring.
* `+ЛІк` is a denominalizer<cite>RW §2.11.1</cite> that has a variety of uses, none of which seem to indicate the possessive relationship that meaning "my heart" would expect. From [sozdik.kz](https://sozdik.kz/ru/dictionary/translate/kk/ru/%D0%BC%D0%B5%D0%BD%D0%B4%D1%96%D0%BA/), one of the definitons is _свойственно только мне_, more or less "mine alone". I think it is being used here to express a more personal ownership than what the possessive pronoun might indicate (one of the examples given is `мендік бол:my soul`.)

# мұз

http://engime.org/kesibi-aza-tili-penin-oitu-edistemelik-keshen.html?page=7

Есте сақтаңыз.

В казахском языке суффиксы -тағы, -тегі, -дағы, -дегі присоединяются к именам существительным, образуя имена прилагательные находящихся в местном падеже, и переводятся как «тот, который находится», «тот, который принадлежит»: компьютердегі файлдар (файлы, которые на компьютерах), менің папкамдағы фаилдар (файлы, которые на мойх папках), компьютер үстеліндегі флешка (флешка, которая лежит на компьютерном столе) и т.д. 

Translated with Google Translate:

In the Kazakh language, the suffixes -tags, -tags, -data, -degi are appended to the nouns, forming adjectives in the local case, and translated as "the one that is", "the one that belongs": the computer file (files, which are on computers), the folder folders (files that are on my folders), the computer үstelindeigi flash drive (a flash drive that lies on a computer desk), etc.
